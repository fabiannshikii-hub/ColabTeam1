Bahasa Inggris

Waste is the residual material from an activity that is no longer useful or has economic value. Waste has several general characteristics, including micro-size, dynamic nature, widespread distribution, and long-term impact. Based on the type of compound, waste is divided into three categories: organic, inorganic, and B3 (Hazardous and Toxic Materials). Of these types of waste, the waste I will be observing is organic waste. One type of inorganic waste I will be observing is plastic waste.

Plastic waste is all kinds of plastic products that are no longer used, thrown away, or have no value for normal or special use. Plastic waste is very diverse, from plastic shopping waste, plastic packaging waste and plastic waste for food. There are also many impacts from plastic use, namely ecosystem damage, water and soil pollution, and climate change. Plastic waste is also dangerous for human health because it can contaminate the food chain.

Plastic waste also has many characteristics. These include being lightweight, malleable, and durable, or difficult to decompose. Some plastics are elastic when stretched or don't break easily. Plastics also come in a wide variety of eye-catching colors, including red, white, clear, green, and even patterned ones. Plastic waste is also impact-resistant and rust-resistant.

Given the many potential impacts, we can still utilize plastic to create more attractive products. For example, we can create flowers for room decorations, shopping bags, and so on. We can add creativity to recycling waste, and it can also increase its sales value.
